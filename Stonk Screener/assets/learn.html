Glossary


These terms are part of the vocabulary used by professional investors and analysts in the stock market to understand, evaluate, and discuss publicly traded companies and their securities. Their meanings are essential for investors to understand the outlook of companies in which they might invest.


Symbol (Ticker):

A unique series of letters assigned to a security for trading purposes. It acts as an abbreviation of the company's name. For example, AAPL is the ticker symbol for Apple Inc.

Company Name:

The legal name of the corporation whose shares are traded on the stock market. For instance, "Apple Inc." refers to the company known for its technology products and services.

Sector:

A segment of the economy into which companies are categorized based on their primary business activities. For example, Apple is classified in the Technology sector.


Price & Relevant Statistics:

The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and Nasdaq in the United States trade from 9:30 a.m. to 4 p.m. Eastern Time, with the first trade in the morning creating the opening price for a stock and the final trade at 4 p.m. providing the day's closing price. Markets are also closed on weekends and all federal holidays.


Current Price:

The price at which a stock is trading during the current trading session.

Open:

The price at which a stock first trades upon the opening of an exchange on a trading day.

High:

The highest price at which a stock trades during a single trading day.

Low:

The lowest price at which a stock trades during a single trading day.

Close:

The price of a stock at the end of a trading day (4:00 PM Eastern Time)

Adjusted Close:

The closing price after adjustments for all applicable splits and dividend distributions.

Market Capitalization (Market Cap):

The total market value of a company's outstanding shares of stock, calculated as current stock price times the total number of outstanding shares.

Volume:

The total number of shares that have been traded during a specific time period. Volume is a key indicator of a security's liquidity and the level of activity or interest that traders have in it during a given time frame.

Beta:

A measure of a stock's volatility in relation to the overall market. A beta greater than 1 indicates that the stock is more volatile than the market, while a beta less than 1 indicates that the stock is less volatile.

Daily Percent Change:

The percentage change in the stock's price as compared to the previous day's closing price. It is calculated using the formula [(current price - previous close) / previous close].

Analyst Recommendation:

Financial analysts give recommendations on stocks based on their research, which can be classified as follows:

1. Buy - The stock is expected to significantly outperform the market.

2. Outperform - The stock is expected to outperform the market.

3. Hold - The stock is expected to perform in line with the market.

4. Underperform - The stock is expected to underperform the market.

5. Sell - The stock is expected to significantly underperform the market.


Target High Price:

The highest price level predicted by analysts for the stock.

Target Low Price:

The lowest price level predicted by analysts for the stock.

Target Mean Price:

The average price target set by analysts for the stock.

Number of Analyst Opinions:

The total number of analysts who have provided ratings or forecasts for the stock.
